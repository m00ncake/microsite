Mass texting helps thousands of organizations engage with their contacts in a quick, easy, and low-cost way. Mass text messaging is already a part of life for your contacts, and the following statistics show why it's so effective as a marketing tool:

  • Texts have a 98% open rate.
  • They boast a 45% response rate.
  • 33% of adults prefer texting to all other communications tools.

The writing (or texts, for that matter) is on the wall. Texting is a big part of the life of your contacts.

Also called bulk texting, a wide range of organizations leverage mass text marketing. Employers, restaurants, service companies, churches, and political parties send mass text messages to keep their contacts informed, generate engagement, and strengthen relationships. Even sports teams use mass text messaging to send out game-day traffic information and score updates.

Mass Texting: How to Send Messages

Mass text messages are sent through a bulk Short Message Service (SMS) service provider. These services have significant advantages over messaging apps and other forms of mass marketing; they are simple to use, have friendly pricing options, and offer a variety of features.

Mass text marketing is easy. There are no graphics to design or technical optimizations to make. All you have to do is log in, choose the group of contacts you'd like to send to, compose your message, schedule it, and analyze the results at a later time. Mass Texting Tools

Before you start sending mass text messages, you have to create a database of contacts who've previously given you express permission to text them. Simply having their contact info or their past purchasing history with you do not qualify as consent.

There are several ways to begin building a strong list of contacts who want to hear from you. The best way to build a great list on autopilot is to leverage Keywords and short codes. You can get creative to provide an incentive to join your mass text marketing list.

For instance, a retailer might use a sign near the entrance to advertise a free item: "Text 'SHADES' to 474747 to get a free pair of sunglasses when you check out today." In that example 'SHADES' is the Keyword and 474747 is the short code.

Short codes are five or six digit numbers used to route texts to the right organization. A Keyword can be used only once for each short code. If you are sharing a short code with other organizations (most do), you'll have to find a keyword that no one else is using. Your mass texting platform should make this easy to do.

When someone uses a Keyword to opt-in, your text messaging platform will automatically put that contact inside the group in your account which corresponds with that keyword.

Another way to build your text messaging list is to use a sign-up widget on your website. Some text message marketing services offer a widget builder so that you can easily create, customize, and embed a form on your website or social media page. That way people who are looking at your site or social feed can opt-in to receive the messages which go to your text list. Make sure to let contacts know that this is a better way to ensure that they receive your messages.

You'll also want to cross-promote your text messaging campaigns through the other marketing channels you use, such as email, social media, print ads, etc.
