Why Use Mass Text Messaging Services?

  1. Text messaging is the #1 communication channel in the world.
  2. Most people have their mobile devices with them at all times.
  3. Nearly 90% of consumers respond to a text message within 30 minutes.
  4. SMS technology works even when internet services are unavailable or overloaded.
  5. There are no special technical or design skills required to send a mass text.
  6. At less than a nickel per message, it's one of the most affordable marketing options available today.
  7. Keeps contacts informed, generates engagement, and develops relationships.
  8. Recipients opt-in willingly to messages, which saves marketers from wasting time on cold calling, cold emailing, and other traditional lead generation tactics.

Tips for Writing an Effective Mass Text Message

  • Concise Messages

    You have 160 characters to express yourself. If you can do it in less, even better! Make sure the message is clear and concise, and written in plain English.

  • Strong CTA's

    Calls-to-action let recipients know what is expected of them and what steps they need to take next. Strong calls-to-action encourage engagement!

  • Good Timing

    You don't want to overwhelm your customers with texts, but you also don't want to neglect them. Create a specific schedule, generally during business hours, and stick to it.

  • Add Value

    Ask yourself, "Am I consistently adding value without being intrusive?" Providing useful information, in addition to promotional offers, will create a level of anticipation and surprise.

  • Know Your Clients

    Analyze purchase history and demographics, so you can send the right messages to the right customers. Segment your clients for targeted promotions.

How to Create a Mass Text Messaging Database

Creating a database of contacts that you can send mass texts to is easier than you think. You cannot simply buy a list of cell numbers and send unauthorized texts — you need a marketing list of people that have specifically given you permission to text them. The most simple and effective way to build out your contacts is through keywords and short codes.

How to Use Keywords for Mass Texting

  1. Decide on a simple word to ask potential contacts to text to a short code number.
  2. Register your short code with your mass text messaging service provider as part of a subscription plan or rented by the month.
  3. Get your keyword noticed. Put up a sign with the keyword and shared short code along with a special offer or opportunity as an incentive.
  4. All of the people that text the keyword to your code will be added to your contact list and you can start mass texting them.

Examples

  • A restaurant asks potential customers to "Text PIZZA to 313131 to receive a 10% discount coupon on your next order." "PIZZA" is the Keyword and "313131" is the short code.
  • A faith-based organization asks its congregation to "Text CHURCH to 313131" to get updates on service times and announcements.
  • A large employer encourages job seekers to "Text WORK to 313131" to see current openings and get alerts when new positions become available.

Is Mass Messaging Legal?

Yes, it is legal to send mass text messages. With that said, it is a strictly permission-based activity and subject to a few rules and regulations, some of which include:

  • Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) of 1991: Prevents organizations from sending spam text messages.
  • Mobile Marketing Association's Code of Conduct: Protects consumer privacy and prevents abuse of the communication channel.
  • Cellular Telecommunications Industry Association (CTIA): Prohibits the use of language likely to cause offense and stipulates that businesses and organizations cannot use their mass text messaging service to endorse pornography, profanity, violence, hate speech, or illegal drug use.

Just use common sense and make sure you are only sending messages to contacts who have opted into the service. In addition to consent, be sure to provide a way for them to opt out to maintain a good user experience.

5 Compliance Tips from the CTIA

  1. No Unsolicited Messages
  2. Use Proper Opt-In Methods
  3. Explain You Recurring Text Program
  4. Allow Customers to Opt-Out Easily
  5. Your Content Must Be Appropriate
