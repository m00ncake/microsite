Getting Started with SMS Marketing

Launching your first text marketing program is easy. In fact, it only takes a few minutes. Most small businesses owners and marketers add click-to-join widgets to websites, email signatures, and social media channels. Additionally, some small businesses pair a Keyword with a Short Code to encourage quick text marketing opt-ins.

Keyword

A unique term (comprised of letters and/or numbers) that people use to communicate with your small business. Example: JOIN

Short Code

A five or six-digit number that people typically send a text message to in order to join an SMS service. There are some distinct short code variations including, shared, dedicated, and vanity. Example: 313131

As you know, text marketing is a permission-based activity. Additionally, small businesses are subject to the rules and regulations of the 1991 Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA). These rules and regulations are intended to prevent spamming and protect personal privacy. The Mobile Marketing Association has produced a Code of Conduct that condenses TCPA into a series of best practices for text marketing. Additionally, EZ Texting offers compliance tips for small businesses. 

Furthermore, text marketing for small business is also subject to the rules of the companies that carry and deliver SMS messages. The Cellular Telecommunications Industry Association (CITA) stipulates that SMS marketing must not contain content such as profanity, pornography, or hate speech. This organization will also prevent the delivery of any small business texting campaign that endorses illegal drug use or violence.
