Why Communicate With Employees via SMS

The benefits of Short Message Service (SMS) texting are virtually limitless. Unlike other internal communications channels, texting is direct and personal. Let’s face it, phone calls are missed, and voicemails are ignored. With text marketing, employee communication is fast and personal.

Moreover, 98% of the population has access to a mobile device capable of sending and receiving SMS. There’s a good chance your employees are already using text messaging daily.

Speed: Messages are sent instantaneously to your employees. There’s no wading through email inboxes or relying on your phone calls to go through.

Directness: Messages are sent directly to employee cell phones.

Reporting: Access data related to texts delivered, contact count, and more. Use this information to fine-tune your internal communications strategy.

Other Reasons for Group Texting Employees

More than just speed, directness, and reporting, group texting employees saves time and money when making important company announcements. The fact is, communicating with employees using SMS is more cost-efficient than gathering the workforce together for an announcement, distracting them from their jobs. Announcing a new initiative or social event is often best done via a short, concise text with a link directing employees to a relevant page on your company’s website.

Most SMS platforms allow business owners to sort employees into groups. As such, messages can be sent to groups of remote works and community workers who might not have access to the internet. The same service can be used to advise prospective jobs seekers of new vacancies.

You can also use SMS to develop more authentic relationships with your employees. Try sending motivational texts or words of encouragement to your workforce.
